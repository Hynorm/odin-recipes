Bisi Bele Bath

Description

Bisi bele bath is a traditional South Indian dish made with lentils, rice, vegetables, and spices. It is a hearty and flavorful meal that is often served for breakfast or lunch.

Ingredients Required

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1/2 cup rice
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1/2 teaspoon hing (asafoetida)
  • 2 tablespoons peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons cashews
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 potato, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt to taste

Procedure

  1. Rinse the toor dal and rice in cold water until the water runs clear. This removes any impurities and ensures even cooking.
  2. In a pressure cooker, combine the cleaned dal, rice, turmeric powder, and enough water to cover the ingredients by at least 2 inches. Close the lid and bring to high pressure. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 5 whistles. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then release any remaining manually.
  3. While the rice and dal cook, heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the mustard seeds and let them pop. This adds a pleasant crackling sound and nutty aroma.
  4. Add the dried red chilies and hing (asafoetida) to the hot oil and cook for a few seconds. These spices release their vibrant flavors and aromas quickly.
  5. Add the peanuts and cashews to the pan and continue cooking until the peanuts slightly brown and the cashews soften. This adds a subtle richness and textural contrast.
  6. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened and translucent. Then, add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become slightly mushy and release their juices.
  7. Add the chopped carrots, potatoes, and cilantro, and cook for a couple of minutes to soften the vegetables while retaining a slight bite.
  8. Finally, add the cooked dal and rice mixture to the skillet and stir well to combine. Season with salt to taste, ensuring every bite is pleasantly balanced.
  9. Enjoy your hot Bisi Bele Bath with your favorite chutney or sambar, making it a complete and satisfying meal!
