Dal Makhani

Description

Dal Makhani is a popular Indian dish originating from the Punjab region. It's a flavorful and creamy lentil soup made with black gram lentils (urad dal), kidney beans (rajma), butter, and cream. It's typically served with rice or flatbread like roti or naan.

Ingrediants Required

  • 1 cup black gram lentils (urad dal)
  • 1/2 cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup cream

  1. Soak the lentils and beans overnight.

    Place the urad dal and rajma in a large bowl and cover them with enough water to submerge them completely. Let them soak overnight or for at least 8 hours.

  2. Cook the lentils and beans in a pressure cooker.

    Rinse the soaked dal and rajma thoroughly and transfer them to a pressure cooker. Add 4 cups of water and cook for 2 whistles or until they are tender. Allow the pressure to release naturally before opening the cooker.

  3. Sauté the aromatics.

    In a large pan or kadhai, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent. Add the minced garlic and chopped ginger, and continue to sauté until they are fragrant and lightly browned.

  4. Add the spices and cook.

    Stir in the cumin seeds, coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala. Cook for about a minute, allowing the spices to release their aroma.

  5. Combine the lentils, beans, and spices.

    Add the cooked dal and rajma to the pan and mix well with the spices. Add a little water if the mixture seems too thick. Bring it to a simmer and cook on medium heat for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  6. Incorporate the cream and adjust seasoning.

    Stir in the cream and let it simmer for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Season with salt to taste.

  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

    Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves or a dollop of butter, if desired. Serve hot with rice, roti, or naan for a delightful and satisfying meal!
